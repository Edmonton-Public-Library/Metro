MeCard

The Metro Edmonton Federation of Libraries (MEFOL) has joined together to provide more convenient library services by breaking down the geographic barriers of the library card. This partnership allows patrons to register their library card at melibraries.ca and access printed, audio and visual materials at any of the following municipal libraries: Edmonton Public Library; Fort Saskatchewan Public Library; St. Albert Public Library and Strathcona County Library. Register your card now. For more information contact your local library.

The MeCard allows customers from one library to join, participate, and borrow resources at another library. Think of it like the TAL (The Library of Alberta) but without having to fill out cumbersome paperwork or getting a new library card number. You submit your home library card number and pin, which is then registered at any other affiliated library.

For this to occur a significant amount of cooperation has to take place behind the scenes. The two libraries must agree to the terms of MeCard policies, on a political level, then both ILSs must cooperate to produce and consume information about customers in an ILS agnostic way.

Enter project Metro. Metro is the service that runs on the ILS server and performs the function of negotiating and translating communications between different ILSs. The design is centered around a few simple requests that are initiated by the customer on a website, then translated and sent to the home (host) library, a response is generated, sent back to the website. The website then confirms the transaction is a way that ensures that no identifiable customer information is stored then passes the response on again as another request to the guest library. The guest then creates a new user record in their ILS.
